The Sine Rule.

In any triangle, the ratio of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is the same for all three pairs. A proof in four steps — drag the vertices to convince yourself.

a / sin A
—
b / sin B
—
c / sin C
—

Drag vertices A B C — the three ratios stay equal.

i.

Setup

A triangle, three angles, three sides.
Label the vertices $A$, $B$, $C$. Each side is named after the angle opposite it: side $a$ faces vertex $A$, side $b$ faces $B$, side $c$ faces $C$.
ii.

Construction

Drop a perpendicular from $C$ to side $AB$.
Call its length $h$. This single line splits the triangle into two right-angled triangles — and that is the whole trick.
iii.

Two views of $h$

Express the same height using each base angle.
In the left right-triangle, $\sin A = h/b$, so:
$$h = b\,\sin A$$
In the right right-triangle, $\sin B = h/a$, so:
$$h = a\,\sin B$$
iv.

Equate

The two expressions for $h$ must agree.
Setting them equal and dividing by $\sin A \sin B$:
$$b\,\sin A = a\,\sin B \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B}$$
By symmetry — drop the perpendicular from $A$ or $B$ instead — the same argument gives $c/\sin C$. Hence:
$$\boxed{\;\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}\;}$$
